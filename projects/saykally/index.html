Saykally Group, UC Berkeley

Intern, Physics Based Microsystems

<p class = “body”, style = “margin:auto; padding: 20px”> Iworking with (now Dr.) Tony Rizzuto from the summer of 2015 until early 2017 on studies regarding the effects of solute on the evaporation rate of liquid water. Evaporative cooling rates are determined by performing Raman thermometry on a free falling droplet train generated in vacuum; these can be used to calculate gamma (evaporation coefficient). </p>

My work with the Saykally group resulted in second authorship of a JPCC paper](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/abs/10.1021/acs.jpcc.6b12851).
