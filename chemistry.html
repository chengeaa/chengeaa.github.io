Research

Saykally Group, College of Chemistry, UC Berkeley

I've been working with (now Dr.) Tony Rizzuto since the summer of 2015 on studies regarding the effects of solute on the evaporation rate of liquid water. Evaporative cooling rates are determined by performing Raman thermometry on a free falling droplet train generated in vacuum. Once enough clean and consistent data are collected, evaporation coefficient can be determined by running results through theoretical models using Matlab and Igor. Want to learn more? Physics Based Microsystems, Sandia National Labs

I spent the summer of 2016 working for this group at Sandia's main location on Kirtland Air Force Base in Albuqueque, New Mexico. Was mentored by a pretty cool guy: Dr. Carlos Perez. One project, as discussed in the poster below was the application of anodization for a variety of metal functionalizations. The second was a critical analysis of how electroplating quality was affected by all sorts of parameters involved in the production process, from photolithography to the electroplating bath.
